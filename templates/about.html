Symptoms and Prevention

Apple Scab

Symptoms: Dark, scabby spots on leaves and fruit, causing them to become deformed.

Prevention: Apply fungicides early in the growing season, remove infected leaves, and maintain good air circulation around the trees.

Apple Black Rot

Symptoms: Brownish-black lesions on fruit, causing them to shrivel and become mummified.

Prevention: Remove infected fruit, prune trees to improve air circulation, and apply fungicides during the growing season.

Apple Cedar Apple Rust

Symptoms: Yellow or orange spots on leaves, often with a powdery appearance; may cause premature leaf drop.

Prevention: Remove nearby juniper trees (alternate host), apply fungicides, and practice good garden hygiene.

Apple Healthy

No symptoms observed as the plant is healthy.

No specific prevention measures are required.

Grape Black Measles

Symptoms: Black, angular spots on leaves, which may also appear on fruit and stems; can lead to defoliation and reduced yield.

Prevention: Plant disease-resistant grape varieties, prune to improve air circulation, and apply fungicides preventively.

Grape Healthy

No symptoms observed as the plant is healthy.

No specific prevention measures are required.

Grape Leaf Blight

Symptoms: Brown or reddish-brown spots on leaves, which may expand and lead to leaf death; can affect yield and quality.

Prevention: Remove and destroy infected plant material, practice good garden hygiene, and apply fungicides as needed.

Cotton Army Worm

Symptoms: Large numbers of armyworms feeding on cotton leaves, leading to skeletonized leaves and reduced yield.

Prevention: Monitor fields regularly, use integrated pest management practices, and apply insecticides when necessary.

Cotton Bacterial Blight

Symptoms: Water-soaked lesions on cotton leaves, which may expand and become necrotic; can lead to defoliation and yield loss.

Prevention: Use disease-resistant cotton varieties, practice crop rotation, and manage irrigation to reduce leaf wetness.

Cotton Healthy

No symptoms observed as the plant is healthy.

No specific prevention measures are required.

Cotton Target Spot

Symptoms: Circular lesions with a target-like appearance on cotton leaves, leading to defoliation and reduced yield.

Prevention: Practice crop rotation, remove crop debris, and apply fungicides preventively.

Potato Early Blight

Symptoms: Dark brown lesions with concentric rings on potato leaves, leading to leaf yellowing and defoliation.

Prevention: Plant disease-resistant potato varieties, practice crop rotation, and apply fungicides preventively.

Potato Healthy

No symptoms observed as the plant is healthy.

No specific prevention measures are required.

Potato Late Blight

Symptoms: Water-soaked lesions on potato leaves, which may turn dark and lead to rapid defoliation; can affect tubers and cause yield loss.

Prevention: Monitor fields regularly, practice good garden hygiene, and apply fungicides preventively, especially during periods of high humidity.

Sugarcane Healthy

No symptoms observed as the plant is healthy.

No specific prevention measures are required.

Sugarcane Red Rot

Symptoms: Internal reddish-brown discoloration of sugarcane stalks, leading to rotting and collapse of the plant.

Prevention: Use disease-free seed cane, practice crop rotation, and manage waterlogged conditions to reduce disease incidence.

Sugarcane Rust

Symptoms: Orange or yellowish powdery lesions on sugarcane leaves, leading to reduced photosynthesis and yield loss.

Prevention: Plant disease-resistant sugarcane varieties, practice good field sanitation, and apply fungicides preventively.

Sugarcane Yellow Leaf Syndrome

Symptoms: Yellowing of sugarcane leaves, often accompanied by stunted growth and reduced yield.

Prevention: Practice proper nutrient management, control pests that may vector the disease, and maintain optimal soil and environmental conditions.

Tomato Early Blight

Symptoms: Dark brown lesions with concentric rings on tomato leaves, leading to leaf yellowing and defoliation.

Prevention: Rotate crops, space plants adequately for good air circulation, and apply fungicides preventively.

Tomato Healthy

No symptoms observed as the plant is healthy.

No specific prevention measures are required.

Tomato Late Blight

Symptoms: Water-soaked lesions on tomato leaves, which may turn dark and lead to rapid defoliation; can affect fruit and cause yield loss.

Prevention: Practice good garden hygiene, apply fungicides preventively, and avoid overhead irrigation to reduce leaf wetness.

Tomato Leaf Mold

Symptoms: Pale yellow or brown patches on tomato leaves, often with fuzzy white or gray mold growth on the underside.

Prevention: Provide good air circulation, avoid overhead watering, and use disease-resistant tomato varieties.

Tomato Mosaic Virus

Symptoms: Mottled or streaked patterns on tomato leaves, stunted growth, and distorted fruit; can spread rapidly in warm weather.

Prevention: Use virus-free seedlings, control aphids (vectors of the virus), and practice good garden sanitation.

Tomato Target Spot

Symptoms: Circular lesions with concentric rings on tomato leaves, leading to leaf yellowing and defoliation.

Prevention: Rotate crops, remove and destroy infected plant material, and apply fungicides preventively.

Tomato Yellow Leaf Curl Virus

Symptoms: Yellowing and upward curling of tomato leaves, stunted growth, and reduced fruit yield.

Prevention: Use virus-resistant tomato varieties, control whiteflies (vectors of the virus), and remove infected plants promptly.

About

Crop diseases globally threaten agricultural productivity and food security significantly. To counter these threats, advanced technological solutions are essential. This is an approach for multi-crop disease detection using Convolutional Neural Networks (CNNs) implemented with web technology, aiming to enhance precision agriculture practices. Leveraging the power of deep learning, our work offers a scalable and efficient solution for identifying diseases across various crops. The web-based implementation ensures accessibility and ease of use for farmers and agricultural stakeholders. The motivation for developing a multi-crop disease detection system using CNNs and web technology lies in addressing pressing challenges such as agricultural productivity, food security, limited diagnostic capabilities, and the opportunities presented by technological advancements in AI and web development. This will provide farmers with timely insights to manage and control outbreaks, ultimately contributing to improved crop yields and sustainable agricultural practices. Our model achieves an accuracy of 94.45% on dataset comprises 25 classes of crop diseases, with a total of 5800 images sourced from publicly available repositories on Kaggle. Leveraging the power of deep learning, our CNN model is trained on this diverse dataset to accurately identify disease patterns across various crops. The integration of web technology facilitates the deployment of our solution online, ensuring accessibility and ease of use for farmers and agricultural stakeholders.
